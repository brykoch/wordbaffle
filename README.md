# Wordbaffle

Inspired by the board game version of a word guessing game you love: one player picks a secret five-letter word, hands the phone over, and the other player tries to guess the word in six attempts.

Built to enjoy on Saturday mornings with my daughter. We hope you enjoy it, too!

No accounts. No installs. No ads. Just open and play!

**[Play now](https://brykoch.github.io/wordbaffle/)**

## How to Play

1. **Pick a word** — The word setter searches or scrolls through a curated list and locks in a five-letter word.
2. **Hand it over** — A cover screen hides the word while you pass the phone to the guesser.
3. **Guess** — The guesser has six tries. After each guess, tiles light up:
   - **Green** — right letter, right spot
   - **Yellow** — right letter, wrong spot
   - **Gray** — letter not in the word
4. **Share** — Copy your result as an emoji grid to text to friends.

## Features

- **Grade-level word filtering** — Choose from Grade 2, 3, or 4 difficulty so younger players can join in
- **Hard mode** — Must use revealed hints in subsequent guesses
- **Hint system** — Tap the life preserver for a nudge when you're stuck
- **Dark mode** — Follows your system preference
- **No backend** — Runs entirely in the browser, works offline

## An Homage

Wordbaffle is a love letter to the classic five-letter word guessing genre. If you've ever enjoyed that "one more guess" feeling, this is for you — just with a friend sitting across the table.
